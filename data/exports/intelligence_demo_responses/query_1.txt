QUERY: Which materials companies benefit most from humanoid robotics scaling?

**ROBOTNIK INTELLIGENCE — Materials × Robotics Dependency Analysis**

The humanoid robotics supply chain creates direct demand for three categories of materials entities in the Robotnik universe:

**Tier 1: Permanent Magnet Supply Chain (Critical)**

Every humanoid robot requires 40-60 servo motors, each containing neodymium-iron-boron (NdFeB) permanent magnets. Scaling humanoid production to 100,000+ units annually (as targeted by XPeng IRON and Tesla Optimus) would require 800-1,200 tonnes of NdFeB magnets per manufacturer — a material increase in global demand.

- **Northern Rare Earth (600111 C1)** — China's largest rare earth producer and the dominant supplier of NdFeB magnet precursors. Benefits from both volume growth and pricing power as humanoid demand compounds automotive and wind energy demand.
- **MP Materials (MP)** — The only integrated rare earth mine-to-magnet producer in the Western hemisphere. Its $1.25B Texas magnet manufacturing campus directly targets the gap between Chinese magnet supply and Western robotics demand. MP is the most direct beneficiary of humanoid scaling outside China.
- **Lynas Rare Earths (LYC AU)** — The largest non-Chinese rare earth miner, supplying separated rare earth oxides to Japanese magnet manufacturers (TDK, Shin-Etsu). Benefits from supply diversification as robotics OEMs seek to de-risk Chinese concentration.

**Tier 2: Structural & Advanced Materials**

- **Shin-Etsu Chemical (4063 JP)** — Produces silicon wafers AND rare earth magnets. Dual exposure to semiconductor and robotics demand makes it a unique cross-stack beneficiary.
- **Kyocera (6971 JP)** — Advanced ceramics for robot joints, sensor housings, and semiconductor packaging substrates. Humanoid robots require precision ceramic components for actuator bearings and optical sensors.

**Tier 3: Battery & Energy Materials**

- **Albemarle (ALB)** and **SQM** — Lithium suppliers for robot battery packs. Each humanoid robot requires 2-5 kWh of battery capacity. At 100K units/year, that's 200-500 MWh of annual demand per manufacturer.
