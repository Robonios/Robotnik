QUERY: What are TSMC's critical upstream dependencies in the Robotnik universe?

**ROBOTNIK INTELLIGENCE — TSMC Upstream Dependency Map**

TSMC (TSM) sits at the center of the frontier stack dependency chain. Its upstream dependencies span all four Robotnik sectors:

**Critical Equipment Dependencies (Semiconductors sector):**

| Supplier | Dependency | Criticality |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **ASML (ASML)** | EUV lithography systems — TSMC is ASML's largest customer. Each EUV system costs ~$350M. TSMC's $52-56B 2026 capex includes significant EUV orders. | **CRITICAL — single source** |
| **Applied Materials (AMAT)** | Deposition and etch equipment for gate-all-around (GAA) transistors at 2nm and below. | High |
| **Lam Research (LRCX)** | Etch and deposition for advanced logic and memory integration. | High |
| **KLA Corp (KLAC)** | Process control and metrology — defect inspection critical for sub-3nm yield management. | High |
| **Tokyo Electron (8035 JP)** | Coater/developer systems for photoresist application. | Medium-High |

**Critical Materials Dependencies (Materials sector):**

| Supplier | Dependency | Criticality |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| **Shin-Etsu Chemical (4063 JP)** | 300mm silicon wafers — TSMC consumes ~25% of global 300mm wafer output. | **CRITICAL** |
| **SUMCO (3436 JP)** | 300mm silicon wafers — second-largest wafer supplier to TSMC. | High |
| **Linde (LIN)** | Ultra-high-purity nitrogen, argon, and specialty gases for cleanroom and process chambers. | High |
| **Air Liquide (AI FP)** | Specialty gases including neon for EUV lithography light sources. | High |
| **Entegris (ENTG)** | Advanced materials purification, filtration, and CMP slurries. | Medium-High |

**EDA & IP Dependencies (Semiconductors sector):**

| Supplier | Dependency |
|----------|-----------|
| **Synopsys (SNPS)** | EDA tools for physical design, timing closure, and DFM at 3nm/2nm. |
| **Cadence (CDNS)** | Complementary EDA tools, particularly for custom/analog IP blocks. |

**Key Insight:** TSMC's $165B US investment doesn't just move fabs — it moves the entire upstream dependency chain. Every Arizona fab requires the same ASML tools, Shin-Etsu wafers, and Linde gases. The Robotnik Materials sub-index's +23.63% Q1 return partly reflects the market pricing in this upstream demand pull.
