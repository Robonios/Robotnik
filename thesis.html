Mission Directive

One Strategic Stack

Robotics and semiconductors are converging into a single strategic stack. Tracking them separately misses the dependency chain that determines which systems scale, which bottleneck, and where the value concentrates.

Robotics is becoming the primary demand driver for a specific class of semiconductor: edge inference chips, motor control MCUs, sensor fusion processors, power electronics, and advanced packaging. Semiconductors are the enabling layer without which robotics cannot scale beyond prototypes.

Existing intelligence tools treat these as separate sectors. Chip-only platforms miss the demand signal. Robot-only platforms miss the supply constraint. Neither captures the relationships that actually determine outcomes.

Robotnik exists to map the full stack — from the robot on the factory floor down through its sensors, compute modules, chip designers, packaging houses, and foundries — because the people making the most expensive decisions in this space need to see the whole chain, not half of it.

This thesis guides everything

1.
Definitions:A company is in scope if its products or services are material to the robotics-semiconductor dependency chain.
2.
The Robotnik Index:Tracks the full stack because no existing ETF does. ROBO and BOTZ cover robotics but barely touch semiconductors. SOXX and SMH cover semiconductors but ignore robotics entirely.
3.
Source of truth:Robotnik is the structured intelligence layer for this converging stack — not just a database, not just news, not just research.
4.
Market direction:As robotics moves from prototypes to deployment, the semiconductor supply chain underneath becomes strategic infrastructure. Robotnik maps where this stack is strengthening, where it is brittle, and where bottlenecks and opportunities are forming.
